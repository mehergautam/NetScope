🔍 What is ARP?
ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) maps IP addresses (Layer 3) to MAC addresses (Layer 2) in local networks. Devices need MAC addresses to communicate at the data link layer.
📡 ARP Request (Broadcast)
When Device A wants to communicate with Device B's IP, it broadcasts an ARP request: "Who has 192.168.1.20? Tell 192.168.1.10". All devices receive it.
✅ ARP Reply (Unicast)
Only Device B responds with a unicast ARP reply: "192.168.1.20 is at DD:EE:FF:44:55:66". Device A updates its ARP cache.
